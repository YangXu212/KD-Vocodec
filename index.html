Abstract

The advancement of speech interfaces operating in resource-constrained environments drives the need for neural speech codecs that achieve a critical balance among computational efficiency, minimized bitrate, and low latency. These codecs must also maintain high perceptual quality in adverse acoustic environments, demonstrating robustness to real-world noise and reverberation. In this paper, we propose KD-Vocodec, a novel framework that tackles this challenge via feature-level knowledge distillation for joint speech coding and enhancement. Our approach employs a feature-level knowledge distillation framework to train a compact student encoder that directly reconstructs the intermediate representations of a pre-trained, high-fidelity teacher codec, aligning the student's output features with the teacher's clean-speech embeddings. Operating at a low latency of 30 ms with scalable bitrates from 1 to 6 kbps, and demonstrating superior performance across multiple objective and subjective metrics under strict low-resource constraints, KD-Vocodec ranked 2nd in Track 2 of the ICASSP 2025 Low-Resource Audio Codec (LRAC) Challenge.
